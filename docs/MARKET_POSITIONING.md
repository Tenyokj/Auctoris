# Auctoris Market Positioning

## Decision

The **official Auctoris v1 market** is:

**commercial licensing for creator audio assets**

This means Auctoris is not positioning itself first as:

1. A general licensing layer for everything
2. A code-license marketplace
3. A full mainstream music-rights exchange

Instead, Auctoris starts with a narrower, more executable wedge.

## What counts as a creator audio asset

For Auctoris v1, the ideal assets are:

1. Background tracks for YouTube, Shorts, Reels, and podcasts
2. Podcast intro and outro music packs
3. Beat packs for creator and brand use
4. Sample packs
5. Commercial-use audio loops and short music assets

These are strong candidates because:

1. They are digitally native
2. They are easy to package into metadata and SKU-like license tiers
3. They are commercially meaningful
4. They avoid the heaviest legal complexity of mainstream music-rights management

## Why not code

Code licensing is a valid domain, but it is not the strongest first market for Auctoris.

Reasons:

1. Code licensing already has entrenched legal and cultural norms
2. Developer adoption requires a very different go-to-market strategy
3. The emotional and brand value is weaker for a premium licensing authority story
4. Creator audio assets fit the ERC-1155 access-key model more naturally for v1 commercial demos

## Why not "all music"

Going directly into broad music licensing would likely be too heavy for v1.

That path quickly introduces:

1. Split ownership
2. Publisher rights
3. Label rights
4. Performance rights
5. Collection societies
6. Jurisdiction-specific legal overhead

That is not a good first launch surface.

## Strategic positioning line

The strongest short product description is:

**Auctoris licenses commercial-use creator audio assets for platforms, brands, and digital creators.**

## Target users

The most natural early user groups are:

1. Independent audio creators
2. Small music catalogs for creators
3. Podcast asset publishers
4. Beat and sample pack sellers
5. Creator platforms that need auditable commercial licensing

## Target partner platforms

The most natural integration targets are:

1. Creator platforms
2. Podcast production tools
3. Content marketplaces
4. Media asset platforms
5. Brand-content workflow tools

## Why this wedge is strong

This market is strong for Auctoris because it gives the protocol:

1. A clear product story
2. A visually marketable future frontend
3. Clear asset categories
4. Clear license tiers
5. A realistic path to first integrations

## v1 message discipline

Until the product matures, Auctoris should consistently say:

1. We license creator audio assets
2. We issue licenses as on-chain access keys
3. Platforms can verify rights directly on-chain
4. A hosted API and SDK can sit on top later

Avoid saying:

1. We license everything
2. We replace all music-rights infrastructure
3. We are a universal legal engine for digital IP

Those claims are too broad for an early-stage protocol and weaken credibility.
