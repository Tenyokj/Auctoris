# Auctoris License Terms Matrix

## Purpose

This document defines the recommended product-facing interpretation of the first Auctoris v1 license tiers.

These terms are not a replacement for formal legal drafting.

They are the **clear summary layer** that the future site, catalog, SDK, and partners should display when presenting Auctoris licenses.

## v1 tier set

The official Auctoris v1 license tiers are:

1. `Creator`
2. `Commercial`
3. `Agency`
4. `Enterprise`

## Positioning summary

| Tier | Best for | Typical pricing posture |
| --- | --- | --- |
| `Creator` | Individual creators | Entry-level |
| `Commercial` | Small business and branded content | Standard commercial |
| `Agency` | Studios and client-service teams | Multi-client |
| `Enterprise` | Large organizations and negotiated deals | Custom / premium |

## Tier details

### 1. Creator

**Who it is for**

1. Solo content creators
2. Independent podcasters
3. Small personal channels
4. Individual social-media publishers

**Allowed use summary**

1. Use in the license holder's own monetized content
2. Use in personal YouTube, podcast, Shorts, or social publishing workflows
3. Use in a single creator brand environment

**Restricted use summary**

1. No use for agency client work
2. No sublicense or resale of the asset itself
3. No redistribution as a stock library or downloadable pack
4. No claiming authorship of the original asset

**Recommended on-chain defaults**

1. Usually perpetual
2. Usually non-transferable

### 2. Commercial

**Who it is for**

1. Small businesses
2. Brand content teams
3. Freelancers delivering commercial content for one operating entity
4. Monetized media channels beyond personal creator use

**Allowed use summary**

1. Use in commercial campaigns for one business entity
2. Use in monetized marketing, creator, and promotional content
3. Use in company-owned branded media output

**Restricted use summary**

1. No general redistribution of the source asset
2. No resale as part of another audio library
3. No open-ended multi-client reuse unless the tier explicitly says so
4. No transfer of the license outside the licensed entity unless contractually allowed

**Recommended on-chain defaults**

1. Perpetual or term-based depending on product
2. Usually non-transferable

### 3. Agency

**Who it is for**

1. Agencies
2. Creative studios
3. Production houses
4. Teams that deliver content for multiple clients

**Allowed use summary**

1. Use across multiple client projects
2. Use in managed campaigns produced by the licensed agency
3. Use in studio-level content operations

**Restricted use summary**

1. No resale of the raw asset as a competing library product
2. No sublicensing outside the defined service relationship
3. No white-label redistribution of the original source files
4. No transfer of rights beyond the agency's governed client use unless explicitly negotiated

**Recommended on-chain defaults**

1. Perpetual for one-time agency tiers or renewable for subscription-style agency access
2. Usually non-transferable

### 4. Enterprise

**Who it is for**

1. Large organizations
2. Broad media operations
3. Procurement-driven brand environments
4. Buyers that need negotiated terms or large-scale usage rights

**Allowed use summary**

1. Use according to negotiated commercial scope
2. Use across approved internal teams, campaigns, or territories depending on the agreement
3. Use in higher-trust business and procurement workflows

**Restricted use summary**

1. Still no raw-asset resale unless explicitly negotiated
2. No undefined sublicensing rights by default
3. No assumption that enterprise means unlimited global rights unless legal terms say so

**Recommended on-chain defaults**

1. Often paired with off-chain negotiated terms
2. Can remain non-transferable even when broader operational rights exist

## Quick comparison matrix

| Capability | Creator | Commercial | Agency | Enterprise |
| --- | --- | --- | --- | --- |
| Personal monetized use | Yes | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| Single business use | Limited | Yes | Yes | Yes |
| Multi-client use | No | Usually no | Yes | Negotiated |
| Large procurement flow | No | No | Limited | Yes |
| Raw asset resale | No | No | No | No unless negotiated |
| Default transferability | No | No | No | Usually no |

## Frontend copy guidance

The future frontend should avoid overloading users with legal language on first contact.

For each tier card, show:

1. Tier name
2. One-line audience summary
3. 3 to 4 allowed-use bullets
4. 2 to 3 restricted-use bullets
5. Price
6. Duration if time-limited

## Example short card copy

### Creator

For solo creators and monetized personal channels.

### Commercial

For brands, freelancers, and small business content teams.

### Agency

For agencies and studios producing work for multiple clients.

### Enterprise

For negotiated, large-scale organizational licensing.

## Product policy recommendation

For Auctoris v1, keep the matrix disciplined:

1. Default non-transferable
2. Do not imply copyright transfer
3. Do not imply unlimited universal rights
4. Do not imply sublicensing unless explicitly intended

## Legal note

This matrix is a product summary, not the final legal instrument.

The final legal terms should eventually be drafted and versioned separately, then linked through asset metadata and frontend presentation.
